## 1. Model Overview

- **Model**: two-country open-economy New Keynesian framework with home-country population mass $`1-\gamma`$ and foreign-country mass $`\gamma`$. Countries are symmetric in preferences, technology, price setting, and policy problems, but shocks may differ.
- **Purpose**: analytical comparison of non-cooperative Nash monetary policy and cooperative monetary policy under sticky domestic producer prices, flexible exchange rates, complete international risk sharing, and a cost-push tradeoff generated by time variation in labor market power.
- **Agents**: households consume CES-composite home and foreign goods, supply differentiated labor monopolistically, and trade complete state-contingent securities; final-good firms aggregate intermediate varieties; intermediate-good firms use labor linearly and set prices à la Calvo; monetary authorities choose nominal rates or equivalently output gaps subject to IS and Phillips constraints.
- **Form**: log-linear analytical model around a deterministic steady state. Variables in the sticky-price policy system are lower-case log deviations; the MMB suffix `AL` is treated as an analytical/log-linear entry. Several formulas from the MinerU text have OCR artifacts and are marked `needs_review`.
- **Runtime validation**: not performed.

## 2. Optimization Problems

### 2.1 Home household

The home representative household of type $`h`$ chooses consumption, labor supply, and a state-contingent asset portfolio:

```math
\max_{\{C_t,N_t(h),D_{t+1}\}} E_0\sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t
\left[\frac{C_t^{1-\sigma}}{1-\sigma}-\frac{N_t(h)^{1+\phi}}{1+\phi}\right]
```

subject to

```math
P_t C_t + E_t\{Q_{t,t+1}D_{t+1}\}
= W_t(h)N_t(h)+D_t-T_t+\Gamma_t.
```

Consumption is a Cobb-Douglas composite of home and foreign goods:

```math
C_t \equiv C_{H,t}^{1-\gamma}C_{F,t}^{\gamma}, \qquad
P_t = k^{-1}P_{H,t}^{1-\gamma}P_{F,t}^{\gamma},
\quad k=(1-\gamma)^{1-\gamma}\gamma^\gamma.
```

The household faces labor demand

```math
N_t(h)=\left(\frac{W_t(h)}{W_t}\right)^{-\eta_t}N_t,
```

with a wage markup $`\mu_t^w=1/(\eta_t-1)`$.

### 2.2 Foreign household

The foreign household solves the symmetric problem with starred variables. Complete international Arrow-Debreu markets and the law of one price imply the international risk-sharing condition used below.

### 2.3 Final-good firms

Home final-good firms choose intermediate inputs $`Y_t(f)`$ to maximize profits under CES technology:

```math
Y_t=\left(\int_0^1 Y_t(f)^{(\xi-1)/\xi}\,df\right)^{\xi/(\xi-1)}.
```

### 2.4 Intermediate-good firms

Each intermediate firm uses labor linearly:

```math
Y_t(f)=A_tN_t(f),
```

with labor aggregate

```math
N_t(f)=\left(\frac{1}{1-\gamma}\int_0^{1-\gamma}N_t(h)^{(\eta_t-1)/\eta_t}\,dh\right)^{\eta_t/(\eta_t-1)}.
```

Firms receive a wage-bill subsidy $`\tau`$, face Calvo price rigidity with non-adjustment probability $`\theta`$, and reset $`P^0_{H,t}`$ to maximize the discounted stream of profits subject to demand.

### 2.5 Policy makers

In the Nash case, the home central bank minimizes the domestic quadratic loss taking foreign variables as given. In the cooperative case, the two central banks jointly minimize a weighted world loss. Policy is solved under discretion, so future expectations are taken as given in the period-by-period policy problem.

## 3. First-Order Conditions

- **(F1) Home-good demand**

```math
P_{H,t}C_{H,t}=(1-\gamma)P_tC_t.
```

- **(F2) Foreign-good demand**

```math
P_{F,t}C_{F,t}=\gamma P_tC_t.
```

- **(F3) Stochastic discount factor**

```math
Q_{t,t+1}=\beta\left(\frac{C_{t+1}}{C_t}\right)^{-\sigma}\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t+1}}\right).
```

- **(F4) Nominal-bond Euler equation**

```math
1=\beta R_t E_t\left[\left(\frac{C_{t+1}}{C_t}\right)^{-\sigma}\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t+1}}\right)\right].
```

- **(F5) Labor supply with flexible wage markup**

```math
\frac{W_t(h)}{P_t}=(1+\mu_t^w)N_t(h)^\phi C_t^\sigma.
```

- **(F6) Symmetric labor allocation**

```math
W_t(h)=W_t,\qquad N_t(h)=N_t.
```

- **(F7) International risk sharing**

```math
C_t=C_t^{\ast}.
```

- **(F8) Final-good demand for variety $`f`$**

```math
Y_t(f)=\left(\frac{P_{H,t}(f)}{P_{H,t}}\right)^{-\xi}Y_t.
```

- **(F9) Home producer-price index**

```math
P_{H,t}=\left(\int_0^1 P_{H,t}(f)^{1-\xi}\,df\right)^{1/(1-\xi)}.
```

- **(F10) Real marginal cost**

```math
MC_t=\frac{(1-\tau)(W_t/P_{H,t})}{A_t}
=\frac{(1-\tau)(W_t/P_t)S_t^\gamma}{kA_t}.
```

- **(F11) Calvo reset-price condition**

```math
E_t\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}\theta^j Q_{t,t+j}Y_{t+j}(f)
\left[P^0_{H,t}-(1+\mu^p)P_{H,t+j}MC_{t+j}\right]=0.
```

- **(F12) Flexible-price markup condition**

```math
\frac{P^0_{H,t}}{P_{H,t}}=(1+\mu^p)MC_t.
```

- **(F13) Calvo price-index law**

```math
P_{H,t}=\left[\theta P_{H,t-1}^{1-\xi}+(1-\theta)(P^0_{H,t})^{1-\xi}\right]^{1/(1-\xi)}.
```

- **(F14) Aggregate demand and terms of trade**

```math
Y_t=k^{-1}C_tS_t^\gamma,\qquad S_t=\frac{Y_t}{Y_t^{\ast}}.
```

- **(F15) Consumption as a function of home and foreign output**

```math
C_t=kY_t^{1-\gamma}(Y_t^{\ast})^\gamma.
```

- **(F16) Aggregate production with price dispersion**

```math
Y_t=\frac{A_tN_t}{V_t},\qquad
V_t=\int_0^1\left(\frac{P_t(f)}{P_t}\right)^{-\xi}df\ge 1.
```

- **(F17) Real marginal cost in output form**

```math
MC_t=(1-\tau)k^{\sigma-1}(1+\mu_t^w)A_t^{-(1+\phi)}
Y_t^\kappa(Y_t^{\ast})^{\kappa_0}V_t^\phi.
```

- **(F18) Open-economy slope parameters**

```math
\kappa=\sigma(1-\gamma)+\gamma+\phi=\sigma+\phi-\kappa_0,\qquad
\kappa_0=\gamma(\sigma-1).
```

- **(F19) Domestic flexible-price marginal cost**

```math
\overline{MC}=\frac{1}{1+\mu^p}.
```

- **(F20) Domestic natural output**

```math
\bar{Y}_t=
\left[
\frac{k^{1-\sigma}A_t^{1+\phi}(Y_t^{\ast})^{-\kappa_0}}
{(1-\tau)(1+\mu^w)(1+\mu^p)}
\right]^{1/\kappa}.
```

- **(F21) Global flexible-price natural output**

```math
\bar{\bar{Y}}_t=
\left[
\frac{k^{1-\sigma}A_t^{1+\phi}(\bar{\bar{Y}}_t^{\ast})^{-\kappa_0}}
{(1-\tau)(1+\mu^w)(1+\mu^p)}
\right]^{1/\kappa}
=\bar{Y}_t\left(\frac{\bar{\bar{Y}}_t^{\ast}}{Y_t^{\ast}}\right)^{-\kappa_0/\kappa}.
```

- **(F22) Log-linear aggregate demand**

```math
y_t=c_t+\gamma s_t.
```

- **(F23) Log-linear consumption Euler equation**

```math
c_t=E_t c_{t+1}
-\frac{1}{\sigma}\left(r_t-E_t\pi_{t+1}-\gamma E_t\Delta s_{t+1}\right).
```

- **(F24) Log-linear terms of trade**

```math
s_t=y_t-y_t^{\ast}.
```

- **(F25) Log-linear production**

```math
y_t=a_t+n_t.
```

- **(F26) New Keynesian Phillips curve in marginal cost**

```math
\pi_t=\delta mc_t+\beta E_t\pi_{t+1},\qquad
\delta=\frac{(1-\theta)(1-\beta\theta)}{\theta}.
```

- **(F27) Marginal cost as domestic output gap plus cost-push shock**

```math
mc_t=\kappa\tilde{y}_t+\mu_t^w.
```

- **(F28) Domestic natural output in log deviations**

```math
\bar{y}_t=\kappa^{-1}\left[(1+\phi)a_t-\kappa_0y_t^{\ast}\right].
```

- **(F29) Home IS equation in the domestic output gap**

```math
\tilde{y}_t=E_t\tilde{y}_{t+1}
-\sigma_0^{-1}\left[r_t-E_t\pi_{t+1}-\overline{rr}_t\right],
\qquad \sigma_0=\sigma-\kappa_0.
```

- **(F30) Home Phillips curve in the domestic output gap**

```math
\pi_t=\beta E_t\pi_{t+1}+\lambda\tilde{y}_t+u_t,
\qquad \lambda=\delta\kappa,\quad u_t=\delta\hat{\mu}^w_t.
```

- **(F31) Domestic natural real interest rate**

```math
\overline{rr}_t=\sigma_0E_t\Delta\bar{y}_{t+1}
+\kappa_0E_t\Delta y_{t+1}^{\ast}.
```

- **(F32) Natural terms of trade relation**

```math
s_t=(\tilde{y}_t-\tilde{y}_t^{\ast})+(\bar{y}_t-\bar{y}_t^{\ast})=(\tilde{y}_t-\tilde{y}_t^{\ast})+\bar{s}_t.
```

- **(F33) Nash subsidy condition**

```math
(1-\tau)(1+\mu^w)(1+\mu^p)(1-\gamma)=1.
```

- **(F34) Nash policy loss**

```math
W^H=-(1-\gamma)\frac{\Lambda}{2}E_0\sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t
\left[\pi_t^2+\alpha\tilde{y}_t^2\right],
\quad \Lambda=\frac{\xi}{\delta},\quad \alpha=\frac{\lambda}{\xi}.
```

- **(F35) Nash discretionary optimality condition**

```math
\tilde{y}_t=-\frac{\lambda}{\alpha}\pi_t=-\xi\pi_t.
```

- **(F36) Nash reduced-form inflation and output gap**

```math
\pi_t=\psi u_t,\qquad \tilde{y}_t=-\xi\psi u_t,\qquad
\psi=\left[(1-\beta\rho)+\lambda\xi\right]^{-1}.
```

- **(F37) Nash implementing interest-rate rule**

```math
r_t=\overline{rr}_t+\vartheta E_t\pi_{t+1},\qquad
\vartheta=1+\frac{\xi\sigma_0(1-\rho)}{\rho}>1.
```

- **(F38) Cooperative subsidy condition**

```math
(1-\tau)(1+\mu^w)(1+\mu^p)=1.
```

- **(F39) Cooperative gap definition**

```math
\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t=\tilde{y}_t-\frac{\kappa_0}{\kappa}\tilde{y}_t^{\ast}.
```

- **(F40) Cooperative welfare loss**

```math
W_C=-\frac{1}{2}\Lambda E_0\sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t
\left[
(1-\gamma)\left(\pi_t^2+\alpha\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t^2\right)
+\gamma\left((\pi_t^{\ast})^2+\alpha^{\ast}(\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t^{\ast})^2\right)
-2\Phi\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t^{\ast}
\right],
```

```math
\Phi=\frac{\delta(1-\sigma)\gamma(1-\gamma)}{\xi}.
```

- **(F41) Cooperative home Phillips curve**

```math
\pi_t=\beta E_t\pi_{t+1}+\lambda\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t
+\lambda_0\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t^{\ast}+u_t,\qquad \lambda_0=\delta\kappa_0.
```

- **(F42) Cooperative foreign Phillips curve**

```math
\pi_t^{\ast}=\beta E_t\pi_{t+1}^{\ast}+\lambda^{\ast}\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t^{\ast}
+\lambda_0^{\ast}\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t+u_t^{\ast}.
```

- **(F43) Cooperative optimality condition in global gaps**

```math
\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t=-\xi\pi_t,\qquad
\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t^{\ast}=-\xi\pi_t^{\ast}.
```

- **(F44) Cooperative optimality condition in domestic gaps**

```math
\tilde{y}_t=-\xi\left(\pi_t+\frac{\kappa_0}{\kappa}\pi_t^{\ast}\right),\qquad
\tilde{y}_t^{\ast}=-\xi\left(\pi_t^{\ast}+\frac{\kappa_0^{\ast}}{\kappa^{\ast}}\pi_t\right).
```

- **(F45) Cooperative Taylor-style rule**

```math
r_t=\overline{rr}_t^d+\vartheta E_t\pi_{t+1}
+\frac{\kappa_0}{\kappa}(\vartheta-1)E_t\pi_{t+1}^{\ast}.
```

## 4. Market Clearing & Identities

- Goods-market clearing, home and foreign:

```math
(1-\gamma)Y_t=(1-\gamma)C_{H,t}+\gamma C_{H,t}^{\ast},\qquad
\gamma Y_t^{\ast}=(1-\gamma)C_{F,t}+\gamma C_{F,t}^{\ast}.
```

- Complete pass-through and the law of one price imply a unit CPI real exchange rate:

```math
\frac{E_tP_t^{\ast}}{P_t}=1.
```

- Balanced trade in each country:

```math
P_{H,t}Y_t=P_tC_t,\qquad P_{F,t}^{\ast}Y_t^{\ast}=P_t^{\ast}C_t^{\ast}.
```

- Nominal exchange rate identity:

```math
e_t=e_{t-1}+s_t-s_{t-1}+\pi_t-\pi_t^{\ast}.
```

These identities support the reduced system in (F14), (F15), (F24), and (F32). The foreign economy has symmetric equations with starred variables and population-share substitutions.

## 5. Exogenous Processes

- **(F46) Cost-push shock**

```math
u_t=\rho u_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t,\qquad 0<\rho<1.
```

- **(F47) Foreign cost-push shock**

```math
u_t^{\ast}=\rho u_{t-1}^{\ast}+\varepsilon_t^{\ast}.
```

- **(F48) Technology shocks**

```math
a_t \ \text{and}\ a_t^{\ast} \ \text{enter natural output through (F28) and its foreign analogue.}
```

## 6. Steady-State Solution

This archive entry is a log-linear analytical model. The steady state is the deterministic zero-inflation reference point around which lower-case variables are log deviations.

1. Normalize technology and symmetric output levels: $`A=A^{\ast}=1`$ and, under symmetry, $`Y=Y^{\ast}`$, so $`S=1`$.
2. Consumption risk sharing and balanced trade imply $`C=C^{\ast}`$ and $`Y=k^{-1}CS^\gamma`$, hence $`C=kY`$ when $`S=1`$.
3. With zero domestic inflation and no price dispersion, all reset prices equal the price index, $`V=1`$.
4. The flexible-price markup condition fixes $`MC=(1+\mu^p)^{-1}`$.
5. The Nash steady-state subsidy satisfies (F33); the cooperative steady-state subsidy satisfies (F38). These are different steady-state policy distortions and must not be mixed.
6. The log-linear dynamic variables satisfy $`\bar{x}=0`$ for $`x\in\{y,c,s,n,mc,\pi,\tilde{y},u,a,r-\bar{rr}\}`$ after the chosen deterministic reference values are set.
7. The natural output law (F28) and natural real-rate law (F31) provide the steady-state-consistent mapping from technology and foreign output to the domestic natural level.

No numeric calibration or Dynare `steady_state_model` has been validated for this entry.

## 7. Timing & Form Conventions

- The derivation is in log-linear form. Lower-case variables denote log deviations from steady state, except $`r_t`$, $`\pi_t`$, and related interest/inflation variables, which are inflation/rate deviations in the paper's analytical notation.
- Domestic inflation $`\pi_t`$ is producer-price inflation, not CPI inflation. Optimal policy targets domestic inflation; the exchange rate adjusts under flexible exchange rates.
- $`\tilde{y}_t`$ is the domestic output gap relative to the domestic flexible-price allocation, taking foreign output as given. $`\tilde{\tilde{y}}_t`$ is the output gap relative to the globally flexible-price cooperative allocation.
- The terms of trade are $`S_t=P_{F,t}/P_{H,t}`$ in levels and $`s_t=y_t-y_t^{\ast}`$ in log-linear equilibrium.
- There is no capital stock in this model. Production is linear in labor, so no capital timing convention is required.
- The timing in the IS curve is forward looking: $`r_t-E_t\pi_{t+1}`$ is the expected real interest-rate term, adjusted by the domestic natural real rate.
- The policy problem is discretionary, not commitment-based; the central bank takes future expectations as given.
