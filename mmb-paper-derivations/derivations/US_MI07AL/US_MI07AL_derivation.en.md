# US_MI07AL -- Derivation (Optimization Problems + First-Order Conditions)

> First-pass private archive derivation for MMB model `US_MI07AL`. Runtime validation was not performed. Formula uncertainty is marked `needs_review`.

Source: Fabio Milani (2007), "Expectations, learning and macroeconomic persistence," *Journal of Monetary Economics* 54(7), 2065-2082, DOI `10.1016/j.jmoneco.2006.11.007`.

## 1. Model Overview

- **Model**: A small estimated New Keynesian model with subjective expectations and constant-gain adaptive learning. The MMB `US_MI07AL` entry is the adaptive-learning version associated with Milani (2007).
- **Purpose**: Fit U.S. quarterly output gap, inflation, and nominal interest-rate data while separating mechanical persistence from persistence generated by learning.
- **Core agents/blocks**: Habit-forming households imply an intertemporal IS/Euler equation for the output gap; Calvo price-setting firms with possible indexation imply a Phillips curve; the central bank follows an interest-rate rule; private agents form forecasts from an estimated perceived law of motion.
- **State variables and observables**: The paper uses output gap \(x_t\), inflation \(\pi_t\), nominal interest rate \(i_t\), natural real-rate shock \(r_t^n\), and cost-push shock \(u_t\). The state vector is \(\xi_t=[x_t,\pi_t,i_t,u_t,r_t^n]'\).
- **Form**: Log-linear model with subjective expectations. The implementation cross-check uses `model(linear)` and an adaptive-learning wrapper. The paper-side derivation below does not run Dynare.

## 2. Optimization Problems

### 2.1 Representative Household

The paper gives the log-linearized Euler equation rather than a complete nonlinear household problem. The underlying problem is a standard optimizing household with internal habit formation:

\[
\max_{\{C_t,B_t\}} E_0 \sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t U(C_t-\eta C_{t-1})
\]

subject to the usual intertemporal budget constraint. After log-linearization around the steady state and writing output-gap dynamics in terms of habit-adjusted output, the household block is summarized by the modified IS/Euler equation in Section 3.

### 2.2 Price-Setting Firms

Firms face Calvo-style price rigidity. Non-reoptimizing firms may index prices to lagged inflation, governed by indexation parameter \(\gamma\). The paper provides the resulting New Keynesian Phillips curve directly rather than the full reset-price problem:

\[
\max_{P_t^{\ast}} \widehat{E}_t \sum_{j=0}^{\infty}(\beta\theta)^j \Lambda_{t,t+j}
\left[P_t^{\ast} \prod_{s=1}^{j}\Pi_{t+s-1}^{\gamma}-MC_{t+j}\right]Y_{t+j|t}
\]

The exact reset-price FOC is not printed in the local Markdown; the reduced Phillips curve is the source-stated equilibrium condition. `needs_review`: the mapping from this generic reset-price problem to the source's \(\xi_p[\omega x_t+\cdots]\) coefficient should be source-checked before treating the derivation as reviewed.

### 2.3 Central Bank and Learning Agents

The central bank does not solve an optimization problem in the paper. Monetary policy is represented by an interest-rate feedback rule.

Private agents behave as econometricians. They estimate a perceived law of motion for \(Z_t=[\pi_t,x_t,i_t]'\) using lagged macro variables and contemporaneous structural shocks, then update coefficients with a constant-gain recursive least-squares rule. This is a forecasting rule, not a planner objective.

## 3. First-Order Conditions

- **(F1) Habit-adjusted IS/Euler equation**:

\[
\widetilde{x}_t
= \widehat{E}_t\widetilde{x}_{t+1}
-(1-\beta\eta)\sigma
\left[i_t-\widehat{E}_t\pi_{t+1}-r_t^n\right].
\]

- **(F2) New Keynesian Phillips curve with indexation and habits**:

\[
\widetilde{\pi}_t
= \xi_p\left[
\omega x_t + \left((1-\eta\beta)\sigma\right)^{-1}\widetilde{x}_t
\right]
+\beta\widehat{E}_t\widetilde{\pi}_{t+1}+u_t.
\]

- **(F3) Monetary policy rule, paper baseline**:

\[
i_t=\rho i_{t-1}+(1-\rho)\left(\chi_{\pi}\pi_t+\chi_x x_t\right)+\varepsilon_t.
\]

The MMB implementation replaces this simple paper rule with the Modelbase policy-rule interface, including `interest_` as the monetary-policy shock and a rule coefficient vector from `policy_param.mat`. That replacement is `implementation_cross_check`, not a paper-side equation.

## 4. Market Clearing & Identities

- **(F4) Inflation indexation identity**:

\[
\widetilde{\pi}_t \equiv \pi_t-\gamma\pi_{t-1}.
\]

- **(F5) Habit-adjusted output-gap identity**:

\[
\widetilde{x}_t
\equiv (x_t-\eta x_{t-1})
-\beta\eta\widehat{E}_t(x_{t+1}-\eta x_t).
\]

- **(F6) Perceived law of motion (PLM)**:

\[
Z_t=a_t+b_tZ_{t-1}+c_tu_t+d_tr_t^n+\varepsilon_t,
\qquad
Z_t\equiv[\pi_t,x_t,i_t]'.
\]

- **(F7) Subjective forecast formula**:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\widehat{E}_t Z_T
&=(I_5-b_t)^{-1}(I_5-b_t^{T-t})a_t+b_t^{T-t}E_tZ_t \\
&\quad+\phi_u u_t(\phi_u I_5-b_t)^{-1}(\phi_u^{T-t}I_5-b_t^{T-t})c_t \\
&\quad+\phi_r r_t^n(\phi_r I_5-b_t)^{-1}(\phi_r^{T-t}I_5-b_t^{T-t})d_t .
\end{aligned}
\]

`needs_review`: the source Markdown prints \(I_5\) although \(Z_t\) is defined as three-dimensional; this may reflect the augmented state dimension including \(u_t\) and \(r_t^n\), but the dimensioning should be checked against the PDF or author code.

- **(F8) State-space actual law of motion and measurement equation**:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\xi_t &= A_t+F_t\xi_{t-1}+G_tw_t,\\
Y_t &= H\xi_t,
\end{aligned}
\qquad
\xi_t=[x_t,\pi_t,i_t,u_t,r_t^n]',\quad w_t\sim N(0,Q).
\]

The state-space matrices are time-varying convolutions of structural parameters and current beliefs. The paper estimates the model with a Kalman filter but does not print the entries of \(A_t\), \(F_t\), \(G_t\), or \(H\) in the Markdown.

## 5. Exogenous Processes

- **(F9) Natural real-rate shock**:

\[
r_t^n=\phi^r r_{t-1}^n+v_t^r,\qquad v_t^r\sim iid(0,\sigma_r^2).
\]

- **(F10) Cost-push shock**:

\[
u_t=\phi^u u_{t-1}+v_t^u,\qquad v_t^u\sim iid(0,\sigma_u^2).
\]

- **(F11) Constant-gain coefficient update**:

\[
\widehat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}_t
=\widehat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}_{t-1}
+\overline{g}R_{t-1}^{-1}X_t
\left(Z_t-X_t'\widehat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}_{t-1}\right).
\]

- **(F12) Regressor second-moment update**:

\[
R_t=R_{t-1}+\overline{g}\left(X_{t-1}X_{t-1}'-R_{t-1}\right).
\]

Here \(\widehat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}_t=(a_t',vec(b_t,c_t,d_t)')'\) and \(X_t=\{1,Z_{t-1},u_t,r_t^n\}\). The constant-gain parameter \(\overline{g}\) is estimated jointly with the structural parameters.

## 6. Steady-State Solution

Because this is a log-linear output-gap/inflation/interest-rate model, the relevant no-shock deterministic steady state is normalized at zero deviations:

\[
\bar{x}=0,\qquad \bar{\pi}=0,\qquad \bar{i}=0,\qquad \bar{r}^n=0,\qquad \bar{u}=0.
\]

The transformed variables are also zero in steady state:

\[
\bar{\widetilde{x}}=0,\qquad \bar{\widetilde{\pi}}=0.
\]

The shock innovations have zero mean:

\[
\bar{v}^r=0,\qquad \bar{v}^u=0,\qquad \bar{\varepsilon}=0.
\]

Under constant-gain learning, beliefs need not be at their rational-expectations fixed point in a finite sample. The asymptotic rational-expectations limit is approached as \(\overline{g}\to 0\) and \(t\to\infty\), but the paper estimates finite-sample learning dynamics. `needs_review`: initial beliefs are fixed in the baseline and later estimated from pre-sample data; this archive entry does not reconstruct those initial values.

## 7. Timing & Form Conventions

- **Expectations**: \(\widehat{E}_t\) denotes subjective expectations formed from the PLM; \(E_t\) denotes model-consistent expectations. At time \(t\), agents observe endogenous variables dated \(t-1\), observe shocks dated \(t\), and use coefficient estimates from \(t-1\) for forecasts.
- **Leads/lags**: The source equations use \(x_{t+1}\), \(\pi_{t+1}\), \(x_{t-1}\), \(\pi_{t-1}\), and \(i_{t-1}\). There are no physical stock variables such as capital.
- **Form**: Log-linear. The MMB `.mod` cross-check confirms `model(linear)`.
- **Observed variables**: The paper fits output gap, inflation, and the nominal interest rate. The implementation cross-check adds Modelbase observables such as annualized interest, quarterly inflation, four-quarter inflation, output gap, and output aliases.
- **Runtime validation**: Not performed; Dynare was not run.
