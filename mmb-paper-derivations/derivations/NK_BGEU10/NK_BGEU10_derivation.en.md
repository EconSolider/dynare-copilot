> Runtime validation was not performed.

## 1. Model Overview

- **Model**: `NK_BGEU10`, the continental Europe calibration of Blanchard and Gali (2010), "Labor markets and monetary policy: A New Keynesian model with unemployment."
- **Core mechanism**: a New Keynesian model with a representative household, labor-market hiring frictions, real-wage rigidity, Calvo price setting, and monetary policy. Productivity shocks affect inflation and unemployment when real wages do not move one-for-one with productivity.
- **Agents and blocks**: household, intermediate-good producers with hiring costs, monopolistically competitive final-good firms with Calvo price setting, wage-setting rule, and monetary authority.
- **Archive form**: `model(linear)` in the Rep-MMB implementation. The paper derives nonlinear objects first, then log-linearizes around a zero-inflation steady state. Variables with hats are log deviations, except the unemployment deviation $\hat u_t$ is the deviation of the unemployment rate from its steady value.
- **EU calibration marker**: `NK_BGEU10` uses the sclerotic labor-market calibration with steady unemployment $u=0.10$ and job-finding rate $x=0.25$.

## 2. Optimization Problems

### 2.1 Representative Household

The household contains a continuum of members and maximizes expected utility:

$$
E_0 \sum_{t=0}^{\infty}\beta^t
\left(\log C_t-\chi\frac{N_t^{1+\phi}}{1+\phi}\right),
$$

subject to $0 \le N_t \le 1$ and the economy-wide resource and asset-market constraints. In the linearized equilibrium, the household intertemporal condition is represented by the consumption Euler equation in Section 3.

### 2.2 Intermediate-Good Firms and Hiring

Intermediate-good firms produce with labor only:

$$
X_t(j)=A_t N_t(j),
$$

and employment evolves with current hiring:

$$
N_t(j)=(1-\delta)N_{t-1}(j)+H_t(j).
$$

Beginning-of-period unemployment, aggregate hires, and labor-market tightness are:

$$
U_t=1-(1-\delta)N_{t-1}, \qquad
H_t=N_t-(1-\delta)N_{t-1}, \qquad
x_t=\frac{H_t}{U_t}.
$$

Hiring costs per hire are proportional to productivity:

$$
G_t=A_t B x_t^\alpha.
$$

Firm profit maximization implies a real marginal cost that depends on real wages, current hiring costs, and expected future hiring-cost savings.

### 2.3 Wage Rule and Price Setters

The model allows a wage band generated by employment surplus. For the real-wage-rigidity case used in the monetary-policy analysis, the real wage follows:

$$
W_t=\Theta A_t^{1-\gamma},
$$

where $\gamma \in [0,1]$ measures real-wage rigidity. Final-good firms face Calvo price setting; only a fraction $1-\theta$ can reset prices in a period.

### 2.4 Monetary Authority

The paper studies extreme policies, optimal policy, and optimized simple rules. The Rep-MMB implementation for `NK_BGEU10` uses the optimal-policy linear system, with an auxiliary multiplier-like variable matching the first-order conditions in Section 3.

## 3. First-Order Conditions

The following numbered equations summarize the linear equilibrium and optimal-policy conditions used for the archive entry. They are continuous across Sections 3-5.

- **(F1) New Keynesian Phillips curve in marginal cost**:

$$
\pi_t=\beta E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\}+\lambda \widehat{mc}_t,
\qquad
\lambda=\frac{(1-\beta\theta)(1-\theta)}{\theta}.
$$

- **(F2) Linearized marginal cost before unemployment substitution**:

$$
\widehat{mc}_t=\alpha g\mathcal{M}\hat{x}_t
-\beta(1-\delta)g\mathcal{M}E_t\left\{
(\hat c_t-\hat a_t)-(\hat c_{t+1}-\hat a_{t+1})+\alpha\hat{x}_{t+1}
\right\}
-\Phi\gamma\hat a_t,
$$

with

$$
\Phi=1-(1-\beta(1-\delta))g\mathcal{M}.
$$

- **(F3) Labor-market tightness from employment dynamics**:

$$
\delta\hat{x}_t=\hat n_t-(1-\delta)(1-x)\hat n_{t-1}.
$$

- **(F4) Consumption from the resource constraint**:

$$
\hat c_t=\hat a_t+\xi_0\hat n_t+\xi_1\hat n_{t-1},
$$

where

$$
\xi_0=\frac{1-g(1+\alpha)}{1-\delta g},
\qquad
\xi_1=\frac{g(1-\delta)(1+\alpha(1-x))}{1-\delta g}.
$$

- **(F5) Consumption Euler equation**:

$$
\hat c_t=E_t\{\hat c_{t+1}\}-\left(i_t-E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\}-\rho\right),
\qquad
\rho=-\log\beta.
$$

- **(F6) Exact unemployment Phillips curve**:

$$
\pi_t=\beta E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\}
-\kappa_0\hat u_t+\kappa_L\hat u_{t-1}
\kappa_F E_t\{\hat u_{t+1}\}
-\lambda\Phi\gamma\hat a_t.
$$

The coefficients are:

$$
\kappa_0=\frac{\lambda h_0}{1-u}, \qquad
\kappa_L=-\frac{\lambda h_L}{1-u}, \qquad
\kappa_F=-\frac{\lambda h_F}{1-u},
$$

$$
h_0=\frac{\alpha g\mathcal{M}}{\delta}
\left(1+\beta(1-\delta)^2(1-x)\right)
\beta(1-\delta)g\mathcal{M}(\xi_1-\xi_0),
$$

$$
h_L=-\frac{\alpha g\mathcal{M}}{\delta}(1-\delta)(1-x)
-\beta(1-\delta)g\mathcal{M}\xi_1,
$$

$$
h_F=-\beta(1-\delta)g\mathcal{M}
\left(\frac{\alpha}{\delta}-\xi_0\right).
$$

`needs_review`: the MinerU OCR around the $h_0$ and $h_F$ definitions is readable but should be checked against the PDF before promotion, because the OCR may have dropped grouping around the $\alpha g\mathcal{M}/\delta$ and $g\mathcal{M}$ factors. The Rep-MMB file uses coefficient definitions corresponding to the paper's exact unemployment Phillips curve.

- **(F7) Optimal policy inflation FOC**:

$$
2\pi_t+\zeta_t-\zeta_{t-1}=0.
$$

- **(F8) Optimal policy unemployment FOC**:

$$
2\alpha_u\hat u_t+\kappa_0\zeta_t
-\beta\kappa_L E_t\{\zeta_{t+1}\}
-\beta^{-1}\kappa_F\zeta_{t-1}=0.
$$

The Rep-MMB variable `eta` corresponds to the optimal-policy multiplier block up to a scaling/sign convention.

## 4. Market Clearing & Identities

- **(F9) Beginning-of-period unemployment identity**:

$$
U_t=1-(1-\delta)N_{t-1}.
$$

- **(F10) Hiring identity**:

$$
H_t=N_t-(1-\delta)N_{t-1}.
$$

- **(F11) Labor-market tightness definition**:

$$
x_t=\frac{H_t}{U_t}.
$$

- **(F12) End-of-period unemployment rate**:

$$
u_t=1-N_t.
$$

- **(F13) Aggregate resource constraint with hiring costs**:

$$
C_t=A_t\left(N_t-Bx_t^\alpha H_t\right).
$$

- **(F14) Annualized inflation reporting identity used by Rep-MMB**:

$$
\text{inflation}_t=4\pi_t.
$$

The simplified Rep-MMB `model(linear)` folds (F2)-(F6) into a compact unemployment Phillips curve in `pi`, `uhat`, lagged `uhat`, expected `uhat`, and `a`, plus the optimal-policy multiplier equation.

## 5. Exogenous Processes

- **(F15) Productivity process**:

$$
\hat a_t=\rho_a \hat a_{t-1}+\varepsilon^a_t.
$$

The Rep-MMB implementation writes the innovation with the opposite sign, `a = ra*a(-1) - a_`; this is an implementation sign convention for the named shock and not a separate source equation.

## 6. Steady-State Solution

The model archived here is linearized around a zero-inflation steady state, so the endogenous deviations in the `model(linear)` block have zero steady state:

$$
\bar\pi=\bar{\hat u}=\bar{\hat a}=\bar\zeta=0,
\qquad
\overline{\text{inflation}}=0.
$$

The underlying nonlinear steady-state objects used to build coefficients are:

$$
\mathcal{M}=\frac{\epsilon}{\epsilon-1}, \qquad
\lambda=\frac{1}{12}, \qquad
\rho=-\log\beta,
$$

$$
\delta=\frac{ux}{(1-u)(1-x)}, \qquad
g=Bx^\alpha,
$$

$$
\Phi=1-(1-\beta(1-\delta))g\mathcal{M},
$$

$$
\alpha_u=\frac{\lambda(1+\phi)\chi(1-u)^{\phi-1}}{\epsilon}.
$$

For the Europe calibration:

$$
u=0.10, \qquad x=0.25, \qquad \delta \approx 0.04.
$$

Rep-MMB calibration cross-check values include $\beta=0.99$, $\phi=1$, $\epsilon=6$, $\lambda=1/12$, $\gamma=0.5$, $\alpha=1$, $B=5/42$, and $\rho_a=0.9$.

## 7. Timing & Form Conventions

- **Form**: linearized `model(linear)` representation; no nonlinear Dynare runtime validation was performed.
- **Inflation**: $\pi_t$ is quarterly inflation; `inflation` in the Rep-MMB file is annualized as $4\pi_t$.
- **Unemployment**: $\hat u_t$ is a deviation of the unemployment rate from steady state, not a log deviation. It is related to employment by $\hat u_t=-(1-u)\hat n_t$ in the linearized model.
- **Labor timing**: beginning-of-period unemployment $U_t$ depends on lagged employment $N_{t-1}$ and separations; new hires work in the period they are hired, so $N_t=(1-\delta)N_{t-1}+H_t$.
- **Policy timing**: the optimal-policy FOCs use the multiplier lag $\zeta_{t-1}$ and expected future multiplier $E_t\zeta_{t+1}$. The Rep-MMB implementation represents this with `eta(-1)` and `eta(+1)`.
- **Shock sign**: the implementation's `a = ra*a(-1) - a_` means a positive `a_` lowers productivity; the source process is recorded generically as (F15).
