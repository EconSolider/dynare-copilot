# NK_CGG02 - Derivation (optimization problems + first-order conditions)

> Status: `needs_review`. This first-pass archive entry is source-backed by the MinerU Markdown for Clarida, Gali, and Gertler (2002). Runtime validation was not performed.

Source: Richard Clarida, Jordi Gali, and Mark Gertler, "A simple framework for international monetary policy analysis," Journal of Monetary Economics 49(5), 879-904, 2002. DOI field in the local index: `10.3386/w8870`.

## 1. Model Overview

- **Model**: two-country open-economy New Keynesian framework with complete international risk sharing, home and foreign sticky prices, monopolistic competition in goods markets, flexible monopolistic labor supply, and cost-push shocks generated by exogenous wage-markup variation.
- **MMB implementation scope**: `NK_CGG02` uses the paper-side Nash/discretion log-linear block: domestic and foreign IS equations, Phillips curves, domestic-natural-output definitions, natural real rates, AR processes for technology and cost-push shocks, and Taylor-style interest-rate rules. The paper also derives cooperative-policy formulas; those are source context and not the implemented baseline in the checked `.mod`.
- **Agents**: home and foreign households; final-goods firms; intermediate-goods firms; home and foreign central banks; fiscal authorities that set employment subsidies in the policy steady state.
- **Model form**: `model(linear)` in the MMB implementation cross-check. Lowercase variables are log deviations from the deterministic steady state; output gaps are deviations from natural output concepts.
- **Formula quality**: the main equilibrium equations are readable in the MinerU Markdown. The optimal pricing objective and several later policy-rule coefficients contain OCR artifacts, so first-pass formula status remains `needs_review`.

## 2. Optimization Problems

### Households

Home households choose consumption, labor supply, and state-contingent portfolios. Their composite consumption and CPI are:

$$
C_t \equiv C_{H,t}^{1-\gamma} C_{F,t}^{\gamma},
\qquad
P_t = k^{-1} P_{H,t}^{1-\gamma} P_{F,t}^{\gamma}
    = k^{-1} P_{H,t} S_t^{\gamma},
$$

where $S_t=P_{F,t}/P_{H,t}$ and $k=(1-\gamma)^{1-\gamma}\gamma^\gamma$.

The representative home household maximizes:

$$
E_0 \sum_{t=0}^{\infty} \beta^t
\left[
\frac{C_t^{1-\sigma}}{1-\sigma}
- \frac{N_t(h)^{1+\phi}}{1+\phi}
\right]
$$

subject to the nominal budget constraint and labor-demand schedule:

$$
P_t C_t + E_t\{Q_{t,t+1}D_{t+1}\}
= W_t(h)N_t(h)+D_t-T_t+\Gamma_t,
$$

$$
N_t(h)=\left(\frac{W_t(h)}{W_t}\right)^{-\eta_t}N_t.
$$

### Final-Goods Firms

Final-goods firms aggregate intermediate varieties:

$$
Y_t=\left(\int_0^1 Y_t(f)^{(\xi-1)/\xi}\,df\right)^{\xi/(\xi-1)}.
$$

Profit maximization delivers variety demand and the domestic price index:

$$
Y_t(f)=\left(\frac{P_{H,t}(f)}{P_{H,t}}\right)^{-\xi}Y_t,
\qquad
P_{H,t}=\left(\int_0^1 P_{H,t}(f)^{1-\xi}\,df\right)^{1/(1-\xi)}.
$$

### Intermediate-Goods Firms

Intermediate firms use linear labor technology:

$$
Y_t(f)=A_t N_t(f),
$$

with labor assembled from differentiated household labor:

$$
N_t(f)=\left(\frac{1}{1-\gamma}\int_0^{1-\gamma}
N_t(h)^{(\eta_t-1)/\eta_t}\,dh\right)^{\eta_t/(\eta_t-1)}.
$$

Each intermediate firm receives a wage-bill subsidy $\tau$, minimizes cost, and sets prices with Calvo probability $\theta$ of not reoptimizing.

### Monetary Authorities

The paper derives discretionary optimal policy from a quadratic welfare approximation. The MMB cross-check implements Taylor-style interest-rate rules calibrated to domestic and foreign inflation:

$$
r_t = \phi_\pi \pi_t + \varepsilon^r_t,
\qquad
r_t^{\ast} = \phi_\pi \pi_t^{\ast} + \varepsilon^{r,\ast}_t.
$$

## 3. First-Order Conditions

- **(F1) Home expenditure share for home goods**:

$$
P_{H,t} C_{H,t}=(1-\gamma)P_t C_t.
$$

- **(F2) Home expenditure share for foreign goods**:

$$
P_{F,t} C_{F,t}=\gamma P_t C_t.
$$

- **(F3) Stochastic discount factor**:

$$
\beta\left(\frac{C_{t+1}}{C_t}\right)^{-\sigma}
\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t+1}}\right)=Q_{t,t+1}.
$$

- **(F4) Consumption Euler equation**:

$$
1=\beta R_t E_t\left[
\left(\frac{C_{t+1}}{C_t}\right)^{-\sigma}
\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t+1}}\right)
\right].
$$

- **(F5) Labor supply with flexible wage markup**:

$$
\frac{W_t(h)}{P_t}=(1+\mu_t^w)N_t(h)^\phi C_t^\sigma,
\qquad
\mu_t^w=\frac{1}{\eta_t-1}.
$$

- **(F6) International risk-sharing condition**:

$$
C_t=C_t^{\ast}.
$$

- **(F7) Real marginal cost**:

$$
MC_t=\frac{(1-\tau)(W_t/P_{H,t})}{A_t}
=\frac{(1-\tau)(W_t/P_t)S_t^\gamma}{kA_t}.
$$

- **(F8) Calvo reset-price condition**:

$$
E_t\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}\theta^j Q_{t,t+j}Y_{t+j}(f)
\left[P^0_{H,t}-(1+\mu^p)P_{H,t+j}MC_{t+j}\right]=0.
$$

The source Markdown for the preceding objective has sign/character OCR damage, but the resulting reset condition is readable.

- **(F9) Flexible-price markup condition**:

$$
\frac{P^0_{H,t}}{P_{H,t}}=(1+\mu^p)MC_t.
$$

- **(F10) Sticky-price Phillips curve**:

$$
\pi_t=\beta E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\}+\delta mc_t,
\qquad
\delta=\frac{(1-\theta)(1-\beta\theta)}{\theta}.
$$

- **(F11) Marginal cost in the domestic output gap**:

$$
mc_t=\kappa \tilde{y}_t+\mu_t^w.
$$

- **(F12) Domestic IS curve in output-gap form**:

$$
\tilde{y}_t=E_t\{\tilde{y}_{t+1}\}
-\sigma_0^{-1}\left[
r_t-E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\}-\overline{rr}_t
\right].
$$

- **(F13) Domestic Phillips curve in output-gap form**:

$$
\pi_t=\beta E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\}+\lambda\tilde{y}_t+u_t,
\qquad
\lambda=\delta\kappa.
$$

- **(F14) Nash discretionary targeting condition**:

$$
\tilde{y}_t=-\frac{\lambda}{\alpha}\pi_t=-\xi\pi_t.
$$

- **(F15) Nash reduced-form domestic inflation**:

$$
\pi_t=\psi u_t,
\qquad
\psi=\left[(1-\beta\rho)+\lambda\xi\right]^{-1}.
$$

- **(F16) Nash reduced-form domestic output gap**:

$$
\tilde{y}_t=-\xi\psi u_t.
$$

- **(F17) Nash interest-rate implementation rule** (`needs_review` coefficient in OCR):

$$
r_t=\overline{rr}_t+\vartheta E_t\{\pi_{t+1}\},
\qquad
\vartheta=1+\frac{\xi\sigma_0(1-\rho)}{\rho}.
$$

The source line prints the coefficient before expected inflation as an OCR-like `9`; the surrounding definition identifies it as $\vartheta$.

## 4. Market Clearing & Identities

- **(F18) Home-goods market clearing**:

$$
(1-\gamma)Y_t=(1-\gamma)C_{H,t}+\gamma C^{\ast}_{H,t}.
$$

- **(F19) Foreign-goods market clearing**:

$$
\gamma Y_t^{\ast}=(1-\gamma)C_{F,t}+\gamma C^{\ast}_{F,t}.
$$

- **(F20) CPI-based real exchange rate under the law of one price**:

$$
\frac{\varepsilon_t P_t^{\ast}}{P_t}=1.
$$

- **(F21) Balanced trade in the home country**:

$$
P_{H,t}Y_t=P_t C_t.
$$

- **(F22) Balanced trade in the foreign country**:

$$
P^{\ast}_{F,t}Y_t^{\ast}=P_t^{\ast} C_t^{\ast}.
$$

- **(F23) Aggregate demand and terms of trade**:

$$
Y_t=k^{-1}C_t S_t^\gamma,
\qquad
S_t=\frac{Y_t}{Y_t^{\ast}}.
$$

- **(F24) Consumption as a function of home and foreign output**:

$$
C_t=kY_t^{1-\gamma}(Y_t^{\ast})^\gamma.
$$

- **(F25) Aggregate production with price dispersion**:

$$
Y_t=\frac{A_tN_t}{V_t},
\qquad
V_t=\int_0^1\left(\frac{P_t(f)}{P_t}\right)^{-\xi}df\ge 1.
$$

- **(F26) Marginal cost as a function of output levels**:

$$
MC_t=(1-\tau)k^{\sigma-1}(1+\mu_t^w)
A_t^{-(1+\phi)}Y_t^\kappa(Y_t^{\ast})^{\kappa_0}V_t^\phi.
$$

- **(F27) Open-economy marginal-cost elasticities**:

$$
\kappa\equiv\sigma(1-\gamma)+\gamma+\phi=\sigma+\phi-\kappa_0,
\qquad
\kappa_0\equiv\sigma\gamma-\gamma=\gamma(\sigma-1).
$$

- **(F28) Domestic natural output**:

$$
\bar{y}_t=\kappa^{-1}\left[(1+\phi)a_t-\kappa_0y_t^{\ast}\right].
$$

- **(F29) Natural real interest rate**:

$$
\overline{rr}_t=\sigma_0E_t\{\Delta\bar{y}_{t+1}\}
+\kappa_0E_t\{\Delta y^{\ast}_{t+1}\},
\qquad
\sigma_0=\sigma-\kappa_0.
$$

- **(F30) Terms of trade in log deviations**:

$$
s_t=y_t-y_t^{\ast}
=\left(\tilde{y}_t-\tilde{y}_t^{\ast}\right)+\left(\bar{y}_t-\bar{y}_t^{\ast}\right).
$$

- **(F31) Nominal exchange-rate identity**:

$$
e_t=e_{t-1}+s_t-s_{t-1}+\pi_t-\pi_t^{\ast}.
$$

## 5. Exogenous Processes

- **(F32) Cost-push shock**:

$$
u_t=\rho u_{t-1}+\varepsilon_t.
$$

- **(F33) Home technology process used in the implementation cross-check**:

$$
a_t=\rho_a a_{t-1}+\varepsilon^a_t.
$$

- **(F34) Foreign technology process used in the implementation cross-check**:

$$
a_t^{\ast}=\rho_a a_{t-1}^{\ast}+\varepsilon^{a,\ast}_t.
$$

- **(F35) Foreign cost-push process used in the implementation cross-check**:

$$
u_t^{\ast}=\rho_u u_{t-1}^{\ast}+\varepsilon_t^{u,\ast}.
$$

- **(F36) Foreign-output process/shock in the implementation cross-check**:

$$
\tilde{y}_t^{\ast}
=E_t\{\tilde{y}_{t+1}^{\ast}\}
-\sigma_0^{-1}\left[
r_t^{\ast}-E_t\{\pi^{\ast}_{t+1}\}-\overline{rr}_t^{\ast}
\right]
+\varepsilon_t^{y,\ast}.
$$

The paper gives the symmetric foreign block analytically. The extra foreign-output innovation is present in `NK_CGG02_rep.mod` and is therefore recorded only as `implementation_cross_check`.

## 6. Steady-State Solution

The archived MMB implementation is linearized, so the computational steady state is zero for all log-deviation endogenous variables:

$$
y_t=\tilde{y}_t=\bar{y}_t=\pi_t=r_t=\overline{rr}_t=u_t=a_t=0,
$$

and analogously for starred foreign variables. The deterministic gross steady-state objects underlying the linearization satisfy:

$$
S=1,\qquad C=C^{\ast},\qquad \Pi=\Pi^{\ast}=1,\qquad V=1.
$$

Open-economy parameter composites used by the linear block are:

$$
\kappa_0=\gamma(\sigma-1),\qquad
\kappa=\sigma+\phi-\kappa_0,\qquad
\sigma_0=\sigma-\kappa_0,\qquad
\delta=\frac{(1-\theta)(1-\beta\theta)}{\theta},\qquad
\lambda=\delta\kappa.
$$

In the checked implementation cross-check, the calibration is `beta=0.99`, `rhoa=0.9`, `rhou=0`, `sigma=7`, `phi=1`, `theta=0.75`, `gamma1=0.5`, `kappa0=sigma*gamma1-gamma1`, `kappa=sigma+phi-kappa0`, `sigma0=sigma-kappa0`, `delta1=((1-theta)*(1-beta*theta))/theta`, and `lambda=delta1*kappa`.

## 7. Timing & Form Conventions

- The implementation is `model(linear)`; variables are log deviations, not levels.
- Inflation $\pi_t$ is domestic producer-price inflation. CPI inflation is not the policy target in the Nash problem.
- The domestic output gap is $\tilde{y}_t=y_t-\bar{y}_t$, where $\bar{y}_t$ is the domestic flexible-price level conditional on foreign output. The cooperative-policy section instead uses a global-flexible-price gap; that distinction is not promoted into the baseline MMB block.
- The natural real rate $\overline{rr}_t$ is forward-looking and depends on expected changes in domestic natural output and foreign output.
- Foreign variables use a star superscript in the derivation and `star` suffixes in the implementation cross-check.
- Stock-capital timing is not relevant: this model has no capital stock in the extracted paper-side NK block.
- Runtime validation was not performed; no Dynare run was executed.
